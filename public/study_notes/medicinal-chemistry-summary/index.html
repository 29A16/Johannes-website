1.3.4. Dipole-dipole

Many molecules posses a permanent movement, if a drug can align in parallel and opposite orientation to the dipole and still interacts with the target region, both binding and activity of the component are increased. The interaction falls of faster than electrostatic interactions but slower than VDW.
Another kind of dipole interactions are induced interactions, e.g. cation-pi interactions – here an positively charged ammonium group induces polarity in an aromatic ring (concentration of electronegativety in the middle) —> Neurotransmitter acetylcholine.

Cation-pi interaction = quatropole in benzine ring. E.g lysine side chain interacting with triptophane side chain —> 40 kJ/mol is as strong as an ion ion interaction!
